Ze Zhao

I am a postdoctoral fellow studying microbial ecology and environmental microbiology, with a focus on nitrogen cycling in coastal ecosystems and freshwater lakes.

My work combines high-throughput sequencing, metagenomics, metatranscriptomics, phylogenomics, microbial enrichment, and wet-lab experiments to understand microbial community assembly and ecological function in aquatic environments.

About

I received my Ph.D. in Environmental Science and Engineering through the joint program of Zhejiang University and Westlake University from 2020 to 2025. My doctoral research explored assembly mechanisms of microbial communities and the ecological functions of a novel ammonia-oxidizing archaea clade in intertidal wetlands.

I am currently working on the enrichment and isolation of Nitrosomaritimum-like ammonia-oxidizing archaea from coastal wetlands, with particular interest in their adaptation to oxygen-limited environments.

Research Highlights

  • Discovered a novel ammonia-oxidizing archaea genus, Candidatus Nitrosomaritimum, in intertidal aquifers and showed its genetic potential for urea utilization and anoxic nitrous oxide reduction.
  • Revealed rhythmic patterns and assembly mechanisms of microbial communities in the intertidal groundwater and coastal seawater continuum.
  • Demonstrated that fungi are a critical component of lake microbiota during cyanobacterial harmful algal blooms.
